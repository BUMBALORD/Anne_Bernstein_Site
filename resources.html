EMERGENCY SERVICES

Alameda County Crisis Support

24-hour crisis hotline to offer support to people of all ages and backgrounds during times of crisis, working to prevent the suicide of those who are actively suicidal and to offer hope and caring during times of hopelessness 800-309-2131

John George Psychiatric Hospital

Located in San Leandro, the hospital is part of Alameda Health System, providing psychiatric emergency and acute care services to adults experiencing severe and disabling mental illness and treats all who seek care regardless of their economic or social status. Diagnosis and evaluation and the use of a solution-focused approach are designed to assist in stabilizing patients, achieving personal goals and developing skills and resources to lead to wellness and recovery.



CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Association for Conflict Resolution

Academy of Family Mediators

The Academy provides information about mediation, models and standards of practice, and provides a directory of Advanced Practitioners in Family Mediation. It is part of the Association for Conflict Resolution.

SEEDS: Community Resolution Center

Services that Encourage Effective Dialogue and Solution. Formerly East Bay Community Mediation, serving Berkeley, Oakland, Hayward, Castro Valley and Fremont. It provides assistance in conflict resolution, interventions in restorative justice to schools, workplaces and communities, and trains individuals, organizations, and government agencies in a variety of topics.



FAMILY THERAPY ORGANIZATIONS

American Family Therapy Academy

AFTA is an organization of approximately 700 leading national and international family therapy educators, researchers, clinicians, program directors, and policy makers dedicated to advancing systemic and social constructionist theory, research, and practice for families in their communities and larger social contexts.

Association for Family Therapy of Northern California

The AFTNC was established in the early 1960s and is the nation's oldest professional association of couples and family therapits, seeking to help families throughout Northern California.



LOW-COST PSYCHOLOGICAL SERVICES

Berkeley Community Mental Health Center

Psychiatrists, psychologists, urses, counselors and others provide direct clinical services. Priority populations served are adults with serious and persistent mental illnesss, adults with major, acute psychiaric concerns, and children and adolescents with serious emotional disturbance.

Psychology Clinic at UC Berkeley

The Psychology Clinic is part of the Clinical Science Program in the Department of Psychology at UC Berkeley, providing mental health services to the Bay Area. The staff includes doctoral students and interns and supervising faculty and clinicians from the community. Offering therapy for individuals, families and couples, assessments and consultation, and community consultation and preventive intervention strategis to schools, mental health and criminal justice agencies and local communities.

Wright Institute Clinics

These training clinics, staffed by Wright Institute doctoral students under faculty supervision, provide low-fee therapy to members of the public. Located on Addison Street in Berkeley, one clinic focuses on long-term psychodynamic treatment. The CBT clinic focuses on long-term and brief cognitive-behavioral treatment. The Assessment Services offers low-fee psychological and neuropsychological testing for children (age 6 and above), adolescents, and adults. The Recovery Clinic focuses individuals challenged by drug addiction and trauma.

Westcoast Children's Clinic

A private, not-for-profit clinic providing individual, group and family therapy for children and their parents, relatives or foster parents from a variety of perspectives with a common commitment to being long-term. A training clinic for psychologists, also does trauma work, psychological evaluations that are collaborative and therapeutic and foster youth development and transition age support groups.

Women's Therapy Center

A non-profit organization in Berkeley, the WTC is dedicated to strengthening families and communities through affordable therapy for multi-cultural and sexually diverse women, adolescent girls (14-18) and couples.
